3. Your Photos and Uploaded Content

🖼️ Your photo is destroyed the moment you download.

When you upload a photo to YAARD, it is processed entirely within your browser using client-side JavaScript. Your image is:

  • Never transmitted to our servers at any point
  • Never stored in any database or cloud storage
  • Never shared with any third party
  • Never used to train AI models
  • Never written to localStorage, sessionStorage, or any browser cache

The only data sent to our AI provider (Anthropic) is the text prompt describing your chosen aesthetic style. Your photo is never included in that request.

What happens when you save your PFP

Whether you use Download PFP, Save for TikTok, or Save for Instagram, the same destruction process fires immediately and automatically:

  1. Your styled PFP is saved to your device as a PNG file.
  2. Both the original and styled image canvases are cleared pixel by pixel using clearRect().
  3. Canvas dimensions are reset to 1×1 pixels, making any residual data inaccessible.
  4. The image object is nulled out and its source reference cleared from JavaScript memory.
  5. The file input is reset, removing the filename reference.

This all happens within 300 milliseconds of your save. A confirmation screen is shown listing exactly what was destroyed.

Important note on platform saves: When you use Save for TikTok or Save for Instagram, YAARD saves the image to your device only — exactly like a normal download. YAARD does not transmit your image to TikTok, Instagram, or any other platform. You then manually upload from your device to whichever platform you choose. Once you upload to a third-party platform, that platform's own privacy policy applies.

In plain terms: after you save your PFP by any method, your original photo no longer exists anywhere in the YAARD session. There is nothing left to access, steal, or leak — because it has been actively wiped, not merely abandoned.
